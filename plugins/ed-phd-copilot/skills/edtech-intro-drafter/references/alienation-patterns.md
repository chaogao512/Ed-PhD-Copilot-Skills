# Alienation Patterns in Education Digitalization

Use these patterns to sharpen the third Introduction paragraph.

| Pattern | Symptom | Researchable contradiction |
|---|---|---|
| Digital formalism | Platforms are used for display, inspection or reporting but not improvement | Digitalization expands visibility without improving governance learning |
| Data islands | Departments collect data separately and cannot coordinate decisions | Data abundance coexists with weak cross-unit governance |
| Teacher digital fatigue | Teachers face repeated platform work and unclear value | Technology adoption increases workload without professional empowerment |
| Algorithmic opacity | Users cannot understand or challenge recommendations | Smart decision support weakens trust and accountability |
| Evaluation score worship | Systems optimize rankings or scores | Measurement displaces developmental evaluation |
| Human agency weakening | AI suggestions become default decisions | Human-in-the-loop is nominal rather than substantive |
| Equity blind spot | Vulnerable groups receive worse recommendations or access | Efficiency goals conflict with educational fairness |

## V1.1 Scenario-Specific Contradictions

| Scenario | Surface narrative | Deeper contradiction to write |
|---|---|---|
| Teacher digital competence | Teachers need more digital training | Training supply increases, but organizational support and classroom transfer remain weak |
| Education data governance | Schools need to connect more data | Data integration expands, but rights, quality, accountability and educational purpose remain unclear |
| Human-AI assessment | AI makes evaluation more efficient | Evaluation efficiency improves, but formative value, teacher judgment and student rights may weaken |
| Smart campus governance | A platform can support whole-campus governance | Technical visibility increases, but cross-department coordination and responsibility allocation lag behind |
| AI-assisted research | AI can accelerate academic work | Efficiency increases, but source verification, authorship responsibility and methodological judgment become fragile |

## Paragraph-Three Diagnostic Questions

Use these questions to turn a broad background into a researchable contradiction:

- Who experiences the problem: teachers, students, administrators, parents, or platform operators?
- Which governance process fails: decision, coordination, feedback, accountability, capacity building, or risk control?
- What does technology make easier, and what educational value does it fail to secure?
- What evidence would show that the contradiction is real?
- What ethical or equity risk appears if the contradiction is ignored?
