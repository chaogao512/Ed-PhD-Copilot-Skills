# Worked Examples for Method Reporting

These examples use the running case: “基于多 Agent 的高校智能校园治理机制研究”.

## 1. Delphi Filled Example

| Item | Filled example |
|---|---|
| Purpose | Validate whether the proposed five-layer smart campus governance architecture is complete, feasible and ethically controllable. |
| Expert criteria | 12-18 experts from educational technology, university administration, data governance, AI ethics and smart campus technical teams. |
| Round 1 | Experts rate necessity and clarity of each architecture layer, actor role and ethical safeguard; open comments collected. |
| Round 2 | Revised items returned with group median and IQR; experts rerate disputed items. |
| Consensus threshold | Retain items with median >= 4 and IQR <= 1 on a 5-point scale; discuss items that fail but are ethically necessary. |
| Output | Final architecture, revised responsibility map, deleted/merged items and unresolved concerns. |

## 2. Questionnaire Filled Example

| Construct | Example operationalization |
|---|---|
| Perceived governance usefulness | “The system helps identify cross-department problems earlier.” |
| Perceived human control | “I can understand, question or override system recommendations.” |
| Trust | “The system's suggestions are transparent enough for decision support.” |
| Workload | “Using the system increases my administrative workload.” |
| Ethical concern | “I worry that student data may be used beyond the original purpose.” |

Minimum reporting: sample roles, item source, reliability, validity route, descriptive statistics and conclusion boundary.

## 3. Interview Filled Example

| Participant | Interview focus |
|---|---|
| School leader | How AI suggestions enter governance meetings and who is accountable |
| Teacher | Whether the mechanism supports or burdens teaching work |
| Student representative | Perceived privacy, fairness and support value |
| Technical staff | Data quality, model monitoring and access control |
| Ethics expert | Risk level, consent, appeal and human oversight |

Coding themes: data interoperability, role responsibility, trust, workload, human override, fairness risk, organizational learning.

## 4. Log/Data Trace Filled Example

| Metric | Source | Claim supported | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Decision latency | Task workflow logs | Governance efficiency | Does not prove educational quality |
| Override rate | Human review records | Human agency | High override may mean either control or poor AI quality |
| Explanation view count | Dashboard logs | Transparency use | Does not prove understanding |
| Cross-department transfer count | Workflow logs | Collaboration intensity | Needs interview evidence to interpret quality |
| Data-quality error rate | Data governance audit | Data reliability | Does not prove ethical safety alone |

## 5. DSR Filled Example

| Stage | Filled example |
|---|---|
| Problem relevance | University smart campus platforms produce fragmented reports but weak cross-department governance learning. |
| Artifact | Five-layer multi-Agent governance mechanism with data governance and human oversight. |
| Design principles | Socio-technical fit, collaborative governance, human-in-the-loop, data minimization. |
| Demonstration | Pilot scenario: academic risk warning and cross-department student support. |
| Evaluation | Delphi expert review, process logs, teacher/administrator questionnaire, interviews, ethics audit. |
| Contribution | Reusable design principles for AI-supported smart campus governance. |
