# Red Lines

Stop and warn when the task asks AI to:

- Fabricate references, policy documents, interview quotes or data.
- Invent results or statistical significance.
- Hide AI involvement where disclosure is required.
- Remove limitations or ethics concerns for rhetorical effect.
- Make final theoretical or methodological judgment without user review.
- Produce high-stakes student or teacher decisions without human oversight.
- List AI as an author or responsible contributor.
- Upload confidential peer-review materials, unpublished manuscripts or identifiable education data to an external AI tool without authorization.
- Claim “no AI was used” when AI materially shaped writing, analysis, coding or figures and disclosure is required.

If a red line appears, provide a compliant alternative workflow.

## EdTech-Specific Red Lines

| Request pattern | Why it is a red line | Compliant alternative |
|---|---|---|
| “Generate real-looking CSSCI references” | Fabricates sources and breaks academic integrity | Create a candidate search table with verification status |
| “Use web snippets as verified Chinese journal citations” | Search snippets do not prove full bibliographic metadata | Keep as candidate and require CNKI, journal-site, DOI, local PDF or school database verification |
| “Use student behavior data to label learning states automatically” | High-risk profiling without context or consent | Design a risk-signal workflow with teacher review and appeal |
| “Remove privacy concerns from the limitations” | Hides material ethical risk | Rewrite limitations with mitigation plan |
| “Prove learning improvement from satisfaction survey” | Evidence overclaiming | Downgrade to perceived usefulness or add outcome evidence |
| “Let AI decide which teachers are digitally competent” | Delegates professional judgment to AI | Use AI to organize evidence; keep expert/peer review human-owned |
| “Do not disclose AI polishing or analysis assistance” | May violate journal, school or funder policy | Check venue policy and write a precise disclosure note |
| “Upload this student interview transcript to an online chatbot” | May disclose sensitive personal or institutional data | De-identify, obtain approval, or use an approved local workflow |
| “Put ChatGPT as a co-author” | AI cannot take responsibility for accuracy and integrity | Acknowledge or disclose tool use according to venue rules |
