# Theory Anchors for EdTech Governance Ideas

Use theory to generate design and evidence, not as decorative background.

| Theory anchor | Best used for | Design implication | Evidence implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Collaborative governance | Multi-actor school, district or university governance | Define shared decision nodes, negotiation routines and accountability | Meeting records, role maps, stakeholder interviews, governance process metrics |
| Socio-technical systems | Smart campus, AI platform and organization redesign | Treat technology and organization as jointly optimized | System logs plus organizational adoption and workflow evidence |
| Organizational change theory | Teacher adoption, leadership and digital transformation | Stage implementation, resistance, capacity building and institutionalization | Longitudinal interviews, adoption metrics, training records |
| Activity theory | Tool-mediated teaching, learning or management practice | Identify subject, object, tools, rules, community and division of labor | Contradiction analysis, observation, artifact analysis |
| TPACK / teacher digital competence | Teacher AI use and professional development | Align technology with pedagogy and content knowledge | Competency rubrics, lesson artifacts, teacher reflection |
| PDCA / continuous improvement | Quality assurance and evaluation loops | Design plan-do-check-act feedback cycles | Before-after process indicators and improvement records |
| Data governance theory | Education data sharing and smart campus governance | Define ownership, standards, quality, authorization and lifecycle | Data policy documents, access logs, quality metrics |
| Human-AI collaboration | Agent systems and decision support | Specify human oversight, explainability, override and accountability | Decision trace, user trust, intervention records, error handling cases |
