# Six-Paragraph Introduction Patterns

| Paragraph | Function | Must include | Common failure |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 Policy demand | Establish strategic and educational context | Policy demand + concrete governance scenario | Empty policy slogan |
| 2 Technology affordance | Explain potential value | Technology affordance + educational mechanism | Technology worship |
| 3 Alienation problem | Focus the contradiction | Specific bottleneck, actor pain point, consequence | Vague “current research is insufficient” |
| 4 Theory bridge | Convert problem into research question | Theory anchor + constructs/mechanism | Decorative theory |
| 5 Proposed response | Preview model/framework/design/evidence | Artifact, mechanism, evidence route, safeguards | Method list without logic |
| 6 Contributions | State claims and structure | 3-4 contributions mapped to sections | Contributions too broad or untestable |

## V1.5 Paragraph Gates

| Paragraph | Extra gate for AI/data/evidence-synthesis papers |
|---|---|
| 1 Policy demand | Translate policy into a concrete governance or risk-control demand |
| 2 Technology affordance | Use conditional language: “may support”, “can provide evidence for”, not “will improve” |
| 3 Alienation problem | Name affected actors and high-risk decision points |
| 4 Theory bridge | Link theory to human oversight, data governance, professional agency or organizational accountability |
| 5 Proposed response | Name evidence route and safeguards; if using literature synthesis, state whether it is systematic, scoping or exploratory |
| 6 Contributions | Separate framework contribution, evidence contribution and governance safeguard contribution |

## Sentence functions

- Background sentence: locates the policy or institutional demand.
- Tension sentence: contrasts digital promise with governance reality.
- Gap sentence: names the conceptual, mechanism or evidence gap.
- Response sentence: names the proposed framework/model/method.
- Boundary sentence: states what the paper does not claim.
- Risk-boundary sentence: states what AI/data systems must not decide without human review.
- Evidence-boundary sentence: states what the literature or empirical evidence can and cannot support.
