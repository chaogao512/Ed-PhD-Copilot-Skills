# Claim-Evidence Matrix

| Claim type | Minimum evidence | Insufficient evidence | Conclusion boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Technical feasibility | System logs, error cases, usability test | Concept diagram only | Can claim feasibility, not educational value |
| Governance efficiency | Process time, decision latency, workflow completion, staff interviews | Model accuracy | Can claim process improvement if measured in real workflow |
| Learning improvement | Learning outcomes, performance tasks, longitudinal traces | Satisfaction only | Avoid causal claim without comparison design |
| Teacher acceptance | Survey, interview, usage trajectory, workload data | Login counts only | Discuss acceptance cautiously |
| Organizational change | Policy/rule changes, routines, capacity building, longitudinal case data | One-time demo | Avoid claiming institutional transformation |
| Equity/fairness | Subgroup analysis, bias audit, accessibility evidence | Overall average | Report uneven effects and mitigation |
| Ethical safety | Consent, anonymization, human review, audit trail | “We value privacy” statement | Safety is a design claim only if safeguards are operational |
| Teacher digital competence | Competence rubric, teaching artifacts, classroom transfer evidence, reflection, peer/expert review | Training hours or login counts only | Can claim participation or exposure, not competence growth |
| Organizational support | Policy/routine changes, leadership support, time/resource allocation, peer learning records, teacher interviews | A training notice only | Can claim support mechanism only when support is enacted |
| Data governance capability | Data ownership, standards, quality process, access control, audit trail, risk register | Data dashboard screenshots | Can claim visualization, not governance capability |
| Human-AI assessment quality | Feedback quality rubric, teacher review logs, student experience, expert rating, correction cases | AI score accuracy only | Can claim scoring performance, not evaluation improvement |
| Systematic evidence claim | Search strategy, inclusion/exclusion criteria, screening record, PRISMA-style flow, appraisal logic | A narrative list of convenient papers | Can claim mapped evidence scope only when search and screening are reproducible |
| Randomized or quasi-experimental effect | Baseline/comparison, assignment or matching logic, attrition, outcome validity, implementation fidelity | Single posttest, satisfaction survey, or unmatched groups | Causal language requires a design that controls plausible rival explanations |
| High-risk education AI safety | Risk classification, human oversight, explainability, appeal/correction route, logging, lifecycle monitoring | A generic ethics paragraph | Can claim governed use only when safeguards are operational and auditable |

## Red-line rules

- Do not use model accuracy to prove education impact.
- Do not infer long-term change from one-time satisfaction data.
- Do not claim causality from cross-sectional survey alone.
- Do not claim fairness from aggregate results alone.
- Do not claim teacher digital competence from training attendance alone.
- Do not claim data governance from platform integration alone.
- Do not claim formative assessment improvement from AI scoring accuracy alone.
- Do not claim systematic review quality without a transparent search, screening and reporting trail.
- Do not claim high-risk AI controllability without human oversight, override and monitoring evidence.

## Claim Downgrade Guide

| If the evidence is only... | Then downgrade the claim to... |
|---|---|
| Usage count, login count, completion certificate | Exposure, participation or adoption trace |
| Satisfaction questionnaire | Perceived usefulness, perceived acceptance or implementation experience |
| Model accuracy, precision, recall or response time | Technical feasibility or prediction performance |
| One expert meeting | Initial content review or face-validity discussion |
| Single case without time series | Context-bound mechanism illustration |
| Aggregate average without subgroup analysis | Overall trend without equity conclusion |
| Convenience literature search | Exploratory background scan |
| AI-generated source list | Candidate bibliography requiring verification |
| High-risk AI prototype without oversight logs | Technical prototype with unresolved governance risk |

## Reporting-Guideline Crosswalk

Use the following external reporting anchors when the claim type requires transparent reporting:

| Claim family | Reporting anchor | Skill rule |
|---|---|---|
| Systematic or scoping review | PRISMA 2020 | Require checklist awareness, flow diagram logic, search strategy, screening criteria and excluded-record accounting. |
| Interviews and focus groups | COREQ | Require researcher role, sampling, interview guide, coding, participant quotation and trustworthiness evidence. |
| Randomized trial or strong intervention evaluation | CONSORT/SPIRIT and WWC standards | Require protocol/design, participant flow, baseline equivalence, attrition, implementation fidelity and outcome validity. |
| Education AI deployment risk | NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles, EU AI Act | Require risk mapping, measurement, management, transparency, robustness, accountability and human oversight. |
