# Worked Examples

## Example 1: Multi-Agent smart campus governance

**Idea**: Build a multi-Agent system to integrate academic affairs, student affairs and teaching quality data for university governance.

**Fatal flaws**: No CRITICAL flaw if the proposal keeps administrators and teachers in the decision loop. Major risks are data access fantasy and ethics.

**Scores**:

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Policy relevance | 5 | Directly addresses education digitalization and smart governance. |
| Educational value | 4 | Could improve support and decision timeliness, but learning value needs evidence. |
| Organizational feasibility | 3 | Requires cross-department data agreements. |
| Human-AI collaboration | 4 | Strong if human review and override are explicit. |
| Methodological evidencability | 4 | Logs, interviews and process metrics are available in a pilot. |
| Ethics controllability | 3 | Student data risks require minimization and approval. |
| Theoretical contribution | 4 | Socio-technical systems and collaborative governance fit well. |

**Verdict**: Proceed with guardrails. Next route: `governance-paper-template`, then `mixed-methods-evidence-template`.

## Example 2: AI teacher digital competence dashboard

**Idea**: Use AI to score teacher digital competence from platform behavior.

**Fatal flaws**: Possible CRITICAL ethics flaw if used for personnel evaluation without consent or human review.

**Repair**: Reframe as formative professional development support. Use TPACK or teacher digital competence framework. Combine logs with self-assessment and interviews.

**Verdict**: Proceed with guardrails only after changing from punitive scoring to developmental feedback.

## Example 3: LLM chatbot for student advising

**Idea**: Let an LLM answer all student academic advising questions.

**Fatal flaws**: Human role erased and ethics/legal risk if advice affects graduation, privacy or mental health decisions.

**Repair**: Position as decision-support triage with human advisor confirmation, transparent uncertainty and escalation rules.

**Verdict**: Pivot unless the design includes human oversight and risk classification.
