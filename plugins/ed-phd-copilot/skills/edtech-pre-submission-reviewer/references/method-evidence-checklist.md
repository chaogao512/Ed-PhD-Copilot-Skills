# Method and Evidence Checklist

- Are research questions answerable by the selected method?
- Is each claim matched to evidence of the same type?
- Are samples, instruments, data sources and analysis steps reported?
- Are reliability, validity, trustworthiness or consensus procedures appropriate?
- Are limitations tied to actual evidence weaknesses?

## Claim downgrade rules

- No control/comparison: avoid strong causal claims.
- No longitudinal data: avoid long-term transformation claims.
- Satisfaction only: avoid learning or governance effectiveness claims.
- Aggregate only: avoid equity and fairness claims.
- Convenience literature search only: avoid systematic-review language.
- AI accuracy only: avoid trustworthy, safe or educationally effective AI claims.

## Causal-Claim Audit

Before accepting words such as “promotes”, “improves”, “enhances”, “causes” or “significantly increases”, check:

1. Is there a baseline or comparison condition?
2. Are groups equivalent or are confounds controlled?
3. Is the outcome a valid measure of the claimed construct?
4. Is the intervention implemented consistently enough to interpret?
5. Is attrition or missing data reported?
6. Are qualitative/process data available to explain how change occurred?

If these are missing, rewrite the claim as perceived usefulness, implementation feasibility, association, mechanism hypothesis or preliminary evidence.

## Reporting-Standard Audit

Use these gates before recommending submission:

| Manuscript evidence type | Required audit | Severity if absent |
|---|---|---|
| Systematic review, scoping review or evidence map | PRISMA-style search strategy, screening flow, inclusion/exclusion criteria and appraisal logic | MAJOR; CRITICAL if conclusions depend on a fake systematic claim |
| Interview or focus-group study | COREQ-style reporting of researcher role, sampling, protocol, coding and representative quotations | MAJOR |
| Intervention or quasi-experiment | CONSORT/SPIRIT-style design transparency plus WWC-style checks for baseline equivalence, attrition, valid outcomes and implementation fidelity | MAJOR/CRITICAL depending on causal claim strength |
| Mixed-methods study | Explicit integration point, joint display or cross-evidence interpretation logic | MAJOR |
| Education AI risk study | Risk map, human oversight, explainability, appeal route, monitoring and incident/audit trail | CRITICAL for high-stakes learner or teacher decisions |

## Evidence-Claim Rewrite Rules

| Overclaim | Safer rewrite |
|---|---|
| “显著提升学习成效” with satisfaction or log frequency only | “参与者报告了较高接受度，并形成了进一步验证学习成效的依据” |
| “系统实现教育数据治理” with dashboards only | “系统实现了数据汇聚与可视化，尚需补充权责、标准、质量与审计机制” |
| “AI 评价安全可靠” with accuracy only | “AI 评分表现达到初步可用水平，但安全性仍需人类复核、解释、申诉和公平性证据支持” |
| “系统综述表明” with unsystematic search | “初步文献梳理显示” |
| “干预导致教师能力提升” without comparison/follow-up | “干预后教师自评或样例表现出现改善迹象，因果解释需谨慎” |
