# Worked Example: Multi-Agent Smart Campus Governance Introduction

## Paper positioning

System architecture and governance mechanism paper with mixed-methods validation.

## Six-paragraph outline

1. **Policy and transformation demand**: National and institutional digital transformation requires universities to move from fragmented administration to data-informed, collaborative governance.
2. **Technology affordances**: Multi-Agent systems and large models can integrate heterogeneous data, coordinate tasks and support scenario-aware decision-making.
3. **Alienation and bottleneck**: Existing smart campus platforms often create data islands, duplicate reporting and weak human accountability; technology visibility does not automatically become governance capacity.
4. **Theory bridge and question**: From socio-technical systems and collaborative governance, the key question is how to design a multi-Agent governance mechanism that aligns data, actors, rules and accountability.
5. **Proposed response**: The study proposes a five-layer architecture with human oversight, data governance and feedback loops, then validates it through expert review, pilot logs and stakeholder interviews.
6. **Contributions**: The paper contributes a theory-grounded governance architecture, a human-AI collaboration mechanism, a mixed-evidence validation design and implementation pathways for university smart governance.

## Logic audit

The draft is strong if the evidence route can support claims about governance effectiveness. It must not claim learning improvement unless learning evidence is collected.
