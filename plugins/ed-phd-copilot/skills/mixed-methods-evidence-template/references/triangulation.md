# Triangulation Template

| Research question | Quantitative evidence | Qualitative evidence | System/process evidence | Convergence logic | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Does the mechanism improve governance efficiency? | Decision latency, completion rate | Administrator interviews | Workflow logs | All show shorter, clearer process | Does not prove learning improvement |
| Does the system support teacher agency? | Teacher acceptance survey | Teacher interviews | Override/explanation logs | Teachers report and enact meaningful control | Does not prove long-term professional growth |
| Is the system ethically controllable? | Access audit, incident count | Stakeholder risk perception | Consent and anonymization records | Safeguards are operational | Cannot claim zero risk |
| Does a teacher digital competence programme transfer to practice? | Competence scale, classroom artifact score, follow-up use | Teacher interviews and reflection journals | PD attendance, coaching logs, lesson artifacts | Self-report, artifact and practice traces converge | Does not prove student achievement without outcome evidence |
| Is an education AI tool trustworthy in use? | Error, fairness and override metrics | Teacher/student experience of explanation and appeal | Risk register, human review logs, incident response | Technical, experiential and governance evidence all show controlled use | Does not prove universal safety or legality |

## Interpretation rule

If evidence conflicts, do not average it away. Explain the contradiction and identify which claim must be narrowed.

## Integration Rules

- State whether integration happens at design, analysis or interpretation stage.
- Use a joint display when quantitative and qualitative findings answer the same research question.
- Treat disagreement as data: identify whether it reflects measurement weakness, implementation variation or stakeholder conflict.
- Link every integrated conclusion to a claim boundary from `claim-evidence-matrix.md`.
- For high-risk education AI, include system/process evidence as a third stream; interviews and statistics alone are not enough.
