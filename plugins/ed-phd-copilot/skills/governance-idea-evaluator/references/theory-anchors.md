# Theory Anchors for EdTech Governance Ideas

Use theory to generate design and evidence, not as decorative background.

| Theory anchor | Best used for | Design implication | Evidence implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Collaborative governance | Multi-actor school, district or university governance | Define shared decision nodes, negotiation routines and accountability | Meeting records, role maps, stakeholder interviews, governance process metrics |
| Socio-technical systems | Smart campus, AI platform and organization redesign | Treat technology and organization as jointly optimized | System logs plus organizational adoption and workflow evidence |
| Organizational change theory | Teacher adoption, leadership and digital transformation | Stage implementation, resistance, capacity building and institutionalization | Longitudinal interviews, adoption metrics, training records |
| Activity theory | Tool-mediated teaching, learning or management practice | Identify subject, object, tools, rules, community and division of labor | Contradiction analysis, observation, artifact analysis |
| TPACK / teacher digital competence | Teacher AI use and professional development | Align technology with pedagogy and content knowledge | Competency rubrics, lesson artifacts, teacher reflection |
| PDCA / continuous improvement | Quality assurance and evaluation loops | Design plan-do-check-act feedback cycles | Before-after process indicators and improvement records |
| Data governance theory | Education data sharing and smart campus governance | Define ownership, standards, quality, authorization and lifecycle | Data policy documents, access logs, quality metrics |
| Human-AI collaboration | Agent systems and decision support | Specify human oversight, explainability, override and accountability | Decision trace, user trust, intervention records, error handling cases |

## Expanded Anchors for V1.1 Multi-Case Use

### Teacher Digital Competence and Organizational Support

Use this anchor when a topic concerns teacher digital competence, AI literacy, professional development, school-based training or regional teacher capacity building.

- **Governance shift**: Move from individual skill training to organizational support, including leadership, time allocation, peer learning, resource access, classroom transfer and continuous feedback.
- **Design implication**: Build a multi-level mechanism across education bureau, school, teaching-research group and teacher. Avoid reducing teacher development to platform completion records.
- **Evidence implication**: Combine competence rubrics, teacher survey, interviews, lesson artifacts, teaching reflection and school support records.
- **Failure signal**: The study measures login counts or training hours and claims teacher professional growth.

### Education Data Governance

Use this anchor when a topic involves data sharing, smart campus data centers, student profiles, early warning systems, learning analytics or cross-department decision support.

- **Governance shift**: Treat data as a governed educational resource, not as raw material for dashboards.
- **Design implication**: Define data ownership, stewardship, standards, access control, lifecycle, quality review, risk assessment, audit trail and appeal mechanism.
- **Evidence implication**: Use policy documents, workflow audits, access logs, data quality indicators, stakeholder interviews and risk registers.
- **Failure signal**: The proposal says “connect all data” without explaining minimization, authorization, retention, correction and accountability.

### AI Risk Governance

Use this anchor when AI systems influence teaching, learning, evaluation, student support, teacher management or administrative decisions.

- **Governance shift**: Move from model capability to risk management and human accountability.
- **Design implication**: Identify the use context, affected groups, potential harms, transparency needs, human oversight, monitoring and incident response.
- **Evidence implication**: Require model documentation, decision traces, user explanation records, override logs, subgroup performance, fairness checks and post-deployment monitoring.
- **Failure signal**: The proposal treats an AI recommendation as a final institutional decision.

### Formative Assessment and Human-AI Evaluation

Use this anchor when AI supports classroom observation, automatic feedback, essay scoring, teaching evaluation or student analytics.

- **Governance shift**: Evaluation should support learning and teaching improvement, not only ranking, surveillance or accountability.
- **Design implication**: Specify what AI can preprocess or suggest, what teachers must interpret, how students can understand or contest results, and how feedback enters improvement cycles.
- **Evidence implication**: Use feedback quality ratings, teacher revision logs, classroom artifacts, student experience data and appeal/correction records.
- **Failure signal**: The study claims “AI scoring accuracy proves educational evaluation improvement.”
