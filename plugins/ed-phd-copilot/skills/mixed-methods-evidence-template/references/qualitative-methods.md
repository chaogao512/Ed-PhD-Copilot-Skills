# Qualitative Methods Minimum Reporting

| Method | Use when | Must report |
|---|---|---|
| Semi-structured interview | Understanding perception, mechanism and resistance | Sampling, protocol, coding process, representative quotes |
| Focus group | Exploring shared norms or stakeholder negotiation | Group composition, facilitation, interaction dynamics |
| Observation | Capturing real practice and workflow | Site, duration, observation focus, field-note protocol |
| Document analysis | Studying policy, rules or institutional traces | Corpus, coding scheme, inclusion criteria |
| Expert review | Evaluating framework or artifact | Expert criteria, review questions, revision decisions |
| Algorithmic impact interview | Understanding stakeholder experience of AI recommendations | Affected role, decision context, perceived agency, explanation needs, appeal experience |
| Implementation case study | Explaining how a governance mechanism works in context | Case boundary, actors, routines, documents, observation/interview triangulation |

## Trustworthiness checks

Use at least two where feasible: member checking, peer debriefing, audit trail, double coding, negative case analysis, thick description.

## COREQ-Aligned Reporting Gate

For interview and focus-group evidence, check three domains before treating the qualitative findings as manuscript-ready:

| Domain | Required reporting questions |
|---|---|
| Research team and reflexivity | Who collected the data, what role they had in the setting, and how their position may shape interpretation |
| Study design | Why participants were selected, how many were approached/completed, where and how data were collected, and what protocol guided discussion |
| Analysis and findings | How coding was performed, whether multiple coders or audit trails were used, and which quotations support each theme |

If any domain is missing, use the finding as exploratory insight rather than strong mechanism evidence.
