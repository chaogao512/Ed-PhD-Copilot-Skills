# Design Science Research Reporting for EdTech Governance

Use this when the study builds a governance framework, smart campus architecture, AI tool, indicator system or intervention artifact.

## DSR Minimum Logic

| Stage | Required question | EdTech governance translation |
|---|---|---|
| Problem relevance | What real problem requires an artifact? | Name the school/university governance bottleneck and stakeholder pain |
| Artifact | What is built? | Framework, mechanism, model, process, platform prototype, indicator system |
| Design principles | Why is it designed this way? | Derive principles from theory, policy, ethics and organizational constraints |
| Demonstration | How is it used in context? | Scenario, case, pilot, expert walkthrough, simulated governance process |
| Evaluation | How is utility and risk evaluated? | Expert review, mixed evidence, process metrics, usability, ethics audit |
| Contribution | What knowledge is produced? | Reusable design principles, governance mechanism, evidence framework |
| Communication | Who must understand it? | Education researchers, school leaders, technical teams, policy actors |

## DSR Anti-Patterns

- Describing a system architecture without evaluation.
- Treating screenshots or module lists as research contribution.
- Claiming effectiveness without use-context evidence.
- Ignoring organizational adoption and ethical safeguards.

## DSR Section Template

1. Problem and context.
2. Requirements derived from theory and stakeholders.
3. Artifact design.
4. Demonstration scenario.
5. Evaluation design and results.
6. Design principles and limitations.
