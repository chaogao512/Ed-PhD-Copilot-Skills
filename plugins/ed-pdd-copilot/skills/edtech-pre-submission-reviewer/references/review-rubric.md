# Review Severity Rubric

| Severity | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| CRITICAL | Invalidates the core contribution, creates ethical risk, or would likely cause rejection | No theory for framework, evidence cannot support conclusion, privacy risk unaddressed, fabricated or unverifiable claims |
| MAJOR | Weakens contribution but can be repaired with substantial revision | Policy slogans, unclear stakeholder roles, incomplete method reporting, weak triangulation, overbroad contribution |
| MINOR | Local clarity, style or presentation issue | Vague label, repeated sentence, weak transition, caption too short |

## Output rule

Each finding must include: location, problem, why it matters, repair action, and whether it is feasible within 72 hours.
