# EdTech Introduction Drafter

## Overview

Generate an Introduction outline for education technology and governance papers. Replace the pure technical-defect story with a dialectical narrative: policy and era demands create transformation pressure; digital technology provides potential; shallow or alienated implementation creates governance contradictions; the study earns legitimacy by resolving that contradiction through theory-grounded design or evidence.

## Reference Navigation

- Read `references/four-part-logic.md` before drafting.
- Read `references/paragraph-patterns.md` for the six-paragraph outline.
- Read `references/alienation-patterns.md` when the problem statement is vague.
- Read `references/worked-examples.md` when the user works on smart campus, Agent or governance mechanism topics.

## Required Inputs

Ask for missing items: education/management scenario, core technology or intervention, stakeholder group, governance bottleneck, theory anchor, evidence route and target venue/manuscript type.

## Core Procedure

1. Position the paper type and contribution.
2. Build the four-part logic: policy demand, technology affordance, alienation contradiction, research legitimacy.
3. Expand into six paragraphs: policy demand, affordance, contradiction, theory bridge, proposed response, contributions.
4. Audit whether policy, theory, method and evidence are connected.
5. Flag unsupported claims and evidence gaps.

## Quality Rules

- Do not frame the problem as only algorithm accuracy, latency or model scale.
- Name human and organizational actors.
- Include at least one theoretical anchor.
- Include ethical or governance safeguards when AI or student data are involved.
- Ensure each contribution maps to a later section.

## Output Format

1. Paper positioning
2. Four-part logic chain
3. Six-paragraph Introduction outline
4. Logic-chain audit
5. Missing information and risks
6. Suggested section titles
