# EdTech Introduction Drafter

## Overview

Generate an Introduction outline for education technology and governance papers. Replace the pure technical-defect story with a dialectical narrative: policy and era demands create transformation pressure; digital technology provides potential; shallow or alienated implementation creates governance contradictions; the study earns legitimacy by resolving that contradiction through theory-grounded design or evidence.

## Required Inputs

If missing, ask for the missing item before drafting:

- Education or management scenario
- Core technology or governance intervention
- Main stakeholder group
- Current governance bottleneck or alienation problem
- Theoretical anchor
- Evidence or method route
- Target venue or manuscript type if known

## Four-Part Introduction Logic

### 1. Macro policy and era background

Explain the strategic demand: education digital transformation, high-quality education system, smart education, AI governance, data-driven school improvement, or modernization of education governance. Avoid empty policy slogans; connect policy pressure to a concrete governance scenario.

### 2. Technology-enabled possibility

Explain what the technology could enable in the scenario: data integration, situational awareness, personalized support, collaborative decision-making, formative evaluation, or organizational learning. Keep claims as possibilities unless evidence already exists.

### 3. Real-world alienation and governance contradiction

Diagnose the gap between promise and practice. Common contradictions:

- Digital formalism and platform stacking
- Data islands and fragmented responsibilities
- Teacher digital fatigue and low technology acceptance
- Algorithmic opacity, privacy risk, and profiling anxiety
- Smart systems that weaken human agency
- Evaluation tools that optimize scores but not development

This paragraph is the argumentative center.

### 4. Research legitimacy and contribution

State how the study responds: a framework, model, mechanism, architecture, indicator system, design artifact, or mixed-methods evaluation. Tie the response to theory and evidence. Explain how it avoids shallow technology application.

## Paragraph Outline Template

Return six paragraphs:

1. Policy and transformation demand
2. Technology affordances in the target scenario
3. Alienation, bottleneck, or governance contradiction
4. Theoretical bridge and research question
5. Proposed framework, mechanism, or empirical design
6. Contributions and article structure

## Quality Rules

- Do not frame the problem as only algorithm accuracy, latency, or model scale.
- Name the human and organizational actors.
- Include at least one theoretical anchor.
- Include ethical or governance safeguards when AI or student data are involved.
- Ensure each contribution maps to a later section.

## Output Format

1. Paper positioning
2. Six-paragraph Introduction outline
3. Logic-chain audit
4. Missing information and risks
5. Suggested section titles
